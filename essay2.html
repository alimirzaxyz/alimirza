It's Not a Space Station, It's a Space Home

October 2024

The International Space Station (ISS) is set to retire in 2030, with plans to deorbit it into the Pacific Ocean. But what will come in its place might be just as exciting, if not more.

Vast is a California-based startup aiming to revolutionize space habitation with the first commercial space station. In May 2023, Vast announced their plans for Haven-1, a single-module commercial space station intended to launch into Low Earth Orbit (LEO) by August 2025. By October of this year, Vast unveiled their designs for Haven-1, demonstrating their ambitious concepts for a new way of living in space.

However, with design concepts being unveiled in October 2024, how could they possibly still be aiming to deploy Haven-1 into LEO by their original target of mid-2025?

Strategic partnerships and acquisitions seem to be the move that Vast is making to speed up the process without sacrificing product integrity, quality, and especially safety. In February 2023, Vast acquired Launcher, along with its team of specialists that can help deploy customer payloads from rideshare flights into orbit. Former Launcher CEO, Max Haot, is now CEO at Vast.

In June 2023, Vast partnered with Impulse Space to develop the propulsion system for Haven-1. These thrusters will allow for maneuvering while in orbit.

Haven-1 is designed to accommodate up to four astronauts for up to 40 days. And while the design might make you think it’s a space-resort, its main function will serve as a low-gravity laboratory, but with some added comfort and pleasing aesthetics.

The ISS has been able to host many more astronauts for much longer periods of time, how can Vast compete with that?

Vast’s vision, as in its name, is quite vast and extends beyond Haven-1. The company plans to eventually connect Haven-1 to a larger space station, aiming to create habitats with artificial gravity and other environments that will mitigate the effects of long-term microgravity exposure.
